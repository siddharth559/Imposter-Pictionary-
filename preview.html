1. Game Concept

Imposter Pictionary is a social deduction version of Pictionary. Every turn, one player draws a word and the others try to guess it as fast as possible.

The twist is that one player is secretly the Imposter. Normal players want their drawings to be guessed correctly. The Imposter wants the opposite: when the Imposter is drawing, they score by making players fail to guess the word.

The fun comes from the tension: the Imposter must draw poorly or misleadingly enough to confuse people, but not so obviously that everyone catches them.

Core feeling: “Is this person just bad at drawing, or are they secretly sabotaging the round?”

2. Objective of the Game

The goal is to finish the game with the highest score.

Players can earn points in four ways:

  1. Guessing other players’ drawings correctly and quickly.
  2. Drawing clearly when they are a normal artist.
  3. Confusing others when they are the Imposter artist.
  4. Correctly accusing the Imposter and stealing half of their points.

However, reckless accusations are dangerous. A wrong accusation makes the accuser lose half of their own points.

3. Key Terms

Artist

The player whose turn it is to draw.

Guessers

All players except the current artist.

Imposter

The secret player who scores by making others fail to guess during their own drawing turn.

Cycle

One full rotation where every player has drawn once.

4. Game Setup

  1. All players join the game.
  2. Each player starts with 0 points.
  3. One player is secretly assigned as the Imposter.
  4. Choose the total number of cycles before starting.
  5. The first artist is selected, and the game begins.

Recommended group size is 5–6 players. Four players is playable but makes deduction easier; seven or eight players is more chaotic and social.

Short game: 2 cycles Standard game: 3–4 cycles Long game: 5+ cycles

5. Turn Flow

Each turn lasts exactly 60 seconds.

  1. The artist receives a word to draw.
  2. The 60-second timer starts.
  3. The artist draws while the other players submit guesses.
  4. Correct guessers receive points based on how fast they guessed.
  5. The artist receives points depending on whether they are normal or the Imposter.
  6. After the first full cycle, eligible players may accuse the current artist of being the Imposter.
  7. The next player becomes the artist.
Simple example:

In a 6-player game, one player draws and the other 5 players guess. The turn ends after 60 seconds. Then scoring happens and the next player draws.

6. Guessing Points

Guessers get more points for guessing early. Only the first correct guess by each player counts.

Time of Correct Guess Points for Guesser
0–20 seconds 30 points
20–40 seconds 20 points
40–60 seconds 10 points
No correct guess 0 points
Example:

If the word is “rocket” and a player guesses it correctly at 15 seconds, they get 30 points. If another player guesses it at 42 seconds, they get 10 points. A player who never guesses correctly gets 0.

7. Normal Artist Scoring

When the artist is not the Imposter, they want as many players as possible to guess correctly.

Normal artist score = 10 × number of correct guessers
Example:

There are 6 players. One player is drawing, so 5 players are guessing. If 4 players guess correctly, the normal artist earns:

10 × 4 = 40 points

The 4 correct guessers also receive their own timing-based points.

8. Imposter Artist Scoring

When the Imposter is the artist, their scoring is reversed. The Imposter wants players to fail to guess the word.

Imposter artist score = 20 × number of failed guessers

The Imposter gets double the normal artist value because their role is risky. If they draw too badly, people may accuse them. If they draw too well, they lose the chance to score.

Example:

There are 6 players. One player is drawing, so 5 players are guessing. If only 2 guess correctly and 3 fail, the Imposter earns:

20 × 3 = 60 points

The 2 correct guessers still receive their timing-based guessing points.

9. Accusations

Players can accuse the current artist of being the Imposter, but only under specific conditions.

  • Only the current drawer can be accused.
  • Players cannot accuse non-drawing players.
  • Accusations are locked during the first full cycle.
  • After the first cycle, eligible players can accuse during a drawing turn by typing an accusation in the chatbox.
  • If multiple players accuse, only the fastest correct accusation gets the reward.

Correct Accusation

If the accused drawer is actually the Imposter:

  1. The fastest correct accuser steals 50% of the Imposter’s current total points.
  2. The Imposter loses those points.
  3. A new Imposter is secretly assigned.

Wrong Accusation

If the accused drawer is not the Imposter:

  1. The accuser loses 50% of their own current total points.
  2. Nobody else receives those lost points.
  3. The game continues normally.
Correct accusation example:

The Imposter has 120 points. A player correctly accuses them first. The accuser steals 60 points. The Imposter drops to 60 points and the accuser gains 60 points.

Wrong accusation example:

A player has 100 points and wrongly accuses the current drawer. They lose 50 points and fall to 50 points. Nobody gains those 50 points.

10. Minimum Score Needed to Accuse

To prevent early or careless accusations, a player must have enough points before they can accuse.

Accusations are also locked until every player has drawn once.

Minimum accusation score = 20 × (number of players − 1) × game-length multiplier
Game Length Multiplier
2 cycles 0.75×
3–4 cycles 1×
5+ cycles 1.25×

Round the final threshold to the nearest 10 points.

Standard 3–4 Cycle Game Thresholds

Total Players Minimum Score Needed to Accuse
4 players60 points
5 players80 points
6 players100 points
7 players120 points
8 players140 points
Example:

In a 6-player standard game, the threshold is:

20 × (6 − 1) × 1 = 100 points

So a player needs at least 100 points to accuse after the first full cycle is complete.

12. Winning the Game

The game ends after the pre-decided number of cycles.

The player with the highest total score wins. This may be a strong guesser, a clear artist, a successful Imposter, or someone who caught the Imposter at the perfect moment.

Optional final bonus: If the Imposter remains uncaught at the end of the game, they receive +50 bonus points.
